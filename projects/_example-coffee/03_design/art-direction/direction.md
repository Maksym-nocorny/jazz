# Art direction — ВОВК Coffee

We presented **three** directions; the client chose **A — Craft Editorial**. Logged in DECISIONS.md (D3).

## A — Craft Editorial ✅ chosen
- **Concept:** a roaster's notebook. Warm, tactile, confident. The product and the origin facts
  are the heroes; the layout reads like an independent magazine, not a SaaS template.
- **Type:** display serif **Spectral** (editorial, Cyrillic) paired with grotesk **Golos Text**
  (clean, purpose-built for Cyrillic) for body. Big, quiet headlines; generous measure.
- **Color:** earthy — espresso brown (`action-primary`), cream paper (`surface`), an **ember**
  terracotta accent and a deep **pine** for focus. No purple, ever.
- **Layout:** editorial & **asymmetric** — offset hero, an alternating origin list (not three
  identical cards), a dark espresso "story" band, real product imagery placeholders.
- **Motion:** restrained — subtle reveal on scroll, hover on cards. Nothing decorative.
- **Why it wins:** research showed local competitors look either generic-startup or sterile-minimal;
  a warm editorial voice with transparent origin data is the clearest way to look like a real
  craft roaster and not AI-generated.

## B — Industrial / Brutalist (considered)
- Raw, high-contrast, mono labels, exposed grid. Strong, but reads cold for a warm product and
  risks alienating a broad DTC audience. Good fuel for small accents only.

## C — Nordic Minimal (considered)
- Lots of white, thin type, tiny accents. Clean but **too close to the "AI minimal" default** and
  hard to differentiate from competitors. Rejected for blandness.

## Anti-AI guardrails for this direction (Judge checks against these)
- Avoid: default Inter/Poppins, purple/indigo, glassmorphism, identical 3-card grid, emoji icons,
  uniform radius, low-contrast gray. (For this direction those are all *wrong*.)
- Prefer: Spectral×Golos pairing, ember/espresso/pine palette, asymmetric origin list, dark story
  band, real coffee photography, descriptive Ukrainian microcopy.
