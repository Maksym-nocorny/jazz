# Competitor teardown — UA specialty-coffee market

> Phase 1 · researcher · internal analysis (English; brand terms kept in Ukrainian).
> Date: 2026-05-21. These are **archetypes** drawn from the UA + international DTC specialty scene,
> not live audits. Specific brand names are illustrative; treat all visual claims as **unverified**
> — they describe the *pattern* ВОВК competes against, not a snapshot of any one site on this date.

## Scope
Five archetypes the home-enthusiast and café buyer actually compare ВОВК against:
A) UA craft-roaster-with-personality, B) UA established specialty roaster/retailer,
C) generic Prom.ua / constructor seller, D) international DTC roaster (Blue-Bottle-class),
E) marketplace/supermarket specialty listing.

---

## A — "Craft roaster with personality" (Mad Heads archetype) — *unverified*
- **Positioning:** bold, irreverent local roaster; coffee as lifestyle/attitude, strong social
  presence. Personality-led rather than origin-data-led.
- **Visual language:** loud brand color, expressive display type, illustration/character-driven,
  energetic photography. Often more "merch brand" than "roaster's notebook."
- **Strengths:** memorable, ownable voice; great on Instagram; community pull.
- **Weaknesses:** attitude can crowd out origin transparency; the *coffee data* (farm, altitude,
  process) is often secondary or buried; can read young/loud for café buyers wanting reliability.
- **Differentiate from:** ВОВК is also "a bit wild," but the wildness must serve **craft + honesty**,
  not noise. Lead with origin facts and a calmer, editorial confidence — wolf as *attentive*, not loud.

## B — "Established specialty roaster / retailer" (Svit Kavy archetype) — *unverified*
- **Positioning:** institutional, broad assortment, beans + gear + café presence; "we know coffee."
- **Visual language:** functional commerce layout, dense catalog, classic/neutral palette, lots of
  SKUs and filters. Trustworthy but utilitarian; little editorial point of view.
- **Strengths:** credibility, range, logistics maturity, café/wholesale track record.
- **Weaknesses:** generic e-comm feel; product pages read like a database, not a story; weak emotional
  hook for the home enthusiast who buys on *narrative + freshness*, not breadth.
- **Differentiate from:** ВОВК wins on **curation over catalog** — only 3 featured lots at a time,
  each told like a magazine spread. Small-batch + 48h freshness is the explicit counter-position to
  "big assortment sitting on a shelf."

## C — "Generic Prom.ua / constructor seller" — *unverified*
- **Positioning:** price/availability seller; coffee as commodity. Often resold beans, thin story.
- **Visual language:** template marketplace chrome, cluttered grids, mismatched product photos,
  default system fonts, banner soup, trust-badge clutter. The "ринок" look the client explicitly
  rejected.
- **Strengths:** cheap, fast to stand up, familiar checkout for some UA buyers.
- **Weaknesses:** zero brand equity; looks untrustworthy for a premium product; no origin
  transparency; terrible on mobile narrative.
- **Differentiate from:** this is the **anti-reference**. Every bespoke move (editorial type,
  asymmetric layout, real bean photography, visible origin data) is partly *to not look like this*.

## D — "International DTC roaster" (Blue Bottle archetype) — *unverified*
- **Positioning:** premium minimalist lifestyle; design-forward, subscription-first, brand-as-ritual.
- **Visual language:** lots of white space, refined but often *very* minimal, restrained palette,
  clean grotesk, beautiful photography, polished subscription funnel.
- **Strengths:** aspirational, high trust, excellent UX and subscription mechanics — a real quality bar.
- **Weaknesses:** for a Lviv buyer it's distant (price, shipping, culture); minimalism can read cool
  and corporate; and at home this exact look is now the **"AI-minimal default"** — easy to clone, hard
  to feel local. Origin storytelling sometimes sacrificed to brand polish.
- **Differentiate from:** borrow the *quality bar and subscription clarity*, reject the cool minimalism.
  ВОВК is **warmer, more local, more origin-data-forward** — Ukrainian voice, землиста палітра, paper
  texture, named founder. Local + transparent is the moat against an imported aesthetic.

## E — "Marketplace / supermarket specialty listing" (Rozetka/ATB-specialty archetype) — *unverified*
- **Positioning:** convenience and price; "specialty" as a shelf tag, not a practice.
- **Visual language:** marketplace card UI, star ratings, stock photos, no roaster identity.
- **Strengths:** reach, logistics, trust in the platform.
- **Weaknesses:** total loss of provenance and freshness signal; roast date rarely shown; commodity framing.
- **Differentiate from:** ВОВК's whole pitch — **named farm, altitude, process, roast-within-24h,
  ship-within-48h** — is exactly what this channel destroys. Make freshness + provenance *legible on
  the page* (dates, altitudes, batch size) as concrete proof, not adjectives.

## Cross-cutting design observations (concrete)
- **Origin data is the category's underused asset.** A/B/C/E mostly bury or omit farm/altitude/process.
  Surfacing it as primary content (per copy-deck: "Усе — на упаковці") is genuinely differentiating.
- **The "3 identical feature cards" grid is everywhere** in C and template clones — and is an AI tell.
  ВОВК's asymmetric origin list (D-direction) directly avoids it.
- **Roast/ship dates are rarely shown.** Making "48 годин до вашої чашки" and "до 12 кг за раз"
  concrete, on-page proof beats every competitor's vague "freshly roasted."
- **Cyrillic type is usually an afterthought** — defaults to Arial/Roboto. A deliberate Cyrillic-first
  pairing (Spectral × Golos Text) instantly reads more crafted than A, B, C, E.
- **Mobile-first matters more here than usual** — traffic arrives from Instagram (client-stated).
  Several archetypes are desktop-catalog-first and degrade on phones.

## What to take vs avoid
- **Take:** D's quality bar + subscription clarity; A's memorable personality (dialed to "attentive
  craft"); B's logistics credibility *as proof copy*, not as a dense catalog.
- **Avoid:** C/E commodity-marketplace chrome; D's now-defaulted cool minimalism; A's noise drowning
  the data; generic startup tropes (purple gradient, 3-card grid, default sans).
