# Discovery interview — <Client Name>

> Prepared by the designer, run by the PM with the client (Phase 2). Write answers to
> `03_design/art-direction/interview-notes.md`. Open-ended, non-leading. In the client's language.

## Warm-up
- Tell me about the business in your own words. What are you most proud of?
- If this project goes perfectly, what changes for you in 6 months?

## Audience & goal
- Walk me through your ideal customer's day. When do they need you?
- What do you want a first-time visitor to think, feel, and do?
- What's the one action that matters most on the site?

## Taste & brand (drives art direction)
- Show me 2–3 sites you love — what specifically do you like? (layout, type, color, mood)
- Show me 2–3 you dislike — what's off?
- Pick 3 words for how the site should feel. Now 3 words for how it should NOT feel.
- Any non-negotiables? (logo, colors, fonts, photography, legal)
- Inside vs outside your category — any look you'd love to borrow from?

## Content & proof
- What makes you genuinely different from competitors? Give a concrete example.
- What proof do we have? (reviews, numbers, names, before/after, awards)
- Who writes/owns the content and imagery? What exists today?

## Practical
- Who approves at each step? What's the timeline and any hard dates?
- Languages, accessibility, regional needs?

## Wrap
- Anything I didn't ask that I should have?
